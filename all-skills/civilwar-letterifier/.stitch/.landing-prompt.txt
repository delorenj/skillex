Design a single DESKTOP marketing landing page (1440px wide) for SlowBurns (slowburns.app), a tool that rewrites any ordinary modern message as a solemn, slightly absurd 19th-century American Civil War field dispatch and renders it as a Ken Burns documentary video. The mood is a candlelit field office at dusk seen the way the first ninety seconds of a serious PBS documentary look - reverent, unhurried, cinematic, and dryly witty. The whole page must feel like a physical object resting on a desk: warm aged parchment, ink, candlelight, and a pressed wax seal. All humor comes from treating mundane messages (running ten minutes late, PTO, a blocked ticket) with grave documentary solemnity; the visuals themselves never wink and are never campy or costume-party.

DESIGN SYSTEM (obey exactly):
Canvas is a radial parchment gradient from center-light #f4e8c9 to #ecdcb0 to #dcc488 to edge #cbae6e. Body and UI text is Iron-Gall Ink #2b1a0c (never pure black). Dates, signatures and drop-caps use a deep-sepia Signature Ink #5a2a16. There is EXACTLY ONE chromatic accent, Wax Seal Oxblood #5a2a16 (saturation under 80%), used only for the wax seal, the single primary button, and one hairline emphasis rule per section. Candlelight #ffc470 / #ffaa46 is used ONLY as soft radial glow and inset specular highlights - never as a flat fill, never as a colored glow ring, never a second accent. Deepest shadows, deboss wells, and the footer are Vignette Umber #140a02; text on that dark footer is Warm Bone #e7d6b0. Hairline bezels, section rules, ruled ledger lines and brass catalog plates are Foxed Edge #cbae6e.
Fonts: DISPLAY headlines in Fraunces, a distinctive engraved serif, run at large optical size with weight-driven hierarchy (thin connective words next to heavy load-bearing nouns within one line), left-aligned, tight leading, old-style figures for dates. BODY and captions in Newsreader (a distinctive modern literary serif), ~1.6 leading, max 65 characters per line. METADATA, kickers, catalog numbers and timestamps in Space Mono, uppercase, letter-spaced. The rendered LETTER BODY (and only the letter body) is set in Pinyon Script copperplate - script appears nowhere else. Do NOT use Inter, Roboto, Arial, Helvetica, Open Sans, any system font, or any generic serif (Times, Georgia, Garamond, Palatino).
Cards use a double-bezel: an outer hairline Foxed-Edge shell around an inner Aged-Parchment #ecdcb0 core with a soft candlelight highlight along the top edge, concentric radii, modest ~6px roundness (no pills). A fixed, pointer-events-none paper-grain/film-noise overlay sits over the whole page at ~0.03 opacity. Motion easing is cubic-bezier(0.32,0.72,0,1): scroll-reveals rise 20px while a blur resolves to 0, staggered ~90ms, like ink drying. One perpetual ambient PAGE motion only - a low-saturation candlelight radial pool in the upper-right that breathes slowly; the hero dispatch card additionally runs a true, imperceptible Ken Burns pan/zoom (scale 1.00 to 1.06 over ~24s) as a muted live preview of the video product - this is product imagery, not page chrome. No bouncing chevrons, no "scroll to explore."

THE WAX SEAL DOCTRINE (obey exactly - this is the single biggest costume-party risk):
The wax seal appears in three strictly separated registers and nowhere else. (1) ONE pressable relief seal that animates: the Letterify submit control in section 5 ("press the seal to dispatch") - irregular edge, dual cast shadow, inset candlelight specular, debossed "SB" monogram; it is the only seal that ever moves. (2) Statically-depicted seals that are product imagery, always already pressed and never interactive: the finished dispatch on the hero card, the parchment dispatches in the Examples, and the small type-height seal that stands in for the final period of the hero headline. (3) A small FLAT debossed "SB" monogram glyph as the brand mark in the header wordmark and the footer colophon - flat, never a relief seal. There are no loose or decorative seals anywhere.

PAGE STRUCTURE (top to bottom):
1) HEADER: a thin parchment bar. Left: the wordmark "SlowBurns" in Fraunces beside a tiny FLAT debossed "SB" monogram glyph (register 3 - flat, not a relief seal). Center-right: Space Mono nav links - "Field Manual", "Examples", "Letterify". Far right: the call-to-action "Compose a dispatch" rendered as a GHOST/secondary button (1px Foxed-Edge #cbae6e hairline outline, Iron-Gall text, transparent fill, no Oxblood fill) - the single Oxblood primary button is reserved for the hero, never duplicated here.

2) HERO: asymmetric two-zone split, full viewport height, never centered. LEFT column: a Space Mono eyebrow kicker "FIELD DISPATCH - CORRESPONDENCE, CIVILIAN" struck above a hairline Foxed-Edge rule; then a three-line Fraunces headline that uses INLINE IMAGE-TYPOGRAPHY - small, rounded, cap-height period visuals set BETWEEN words as punctuation, seated in the baseline flow with side gutters, never overlapping the glyphs: "Send word from [inline: a small rounded sepia-duotone oval tintype portrait of a weary bearded officer, behind faint glass] the front - even when the front is a Tuesday stand-up[inline: a small type-height statically-depicted, already-pressed Oxblood wax seal standing in for the final period]." Heavy weight on "Send word" and "front"; thin weight on the connective words. Below: a dry, reverent Newsreader subhead: "SlowBurns rewrites your plainest messages as Civil War field dispatches and reads them aloud as though the Republic hung in the balance - a full documentary treatment for the news that you will be ten minutes late." Then the ONE Oxblood primary button on the page, "Compose a dispatch" (with the nested trailing circle), and a quiet ghost text link "Watch a dispatch." RIGHT column: the actual product shown as a physical object - an Aged-Parchment dispatch card rotated about -1.5 degrees like a letter laid on a desk, with faint Foxed-Edge ruled ledger lines, a Space Mono date in Signature Ink at top, several lines of the letter body in Pinyon Script, a signature in Signature Ink, and a statically-depicted, already-pressed Oxblood wax seal in a debossed well at the lower right (register 2 - it does NOT stamp on load; the card just runs the imperceptible Ken Burns pan/zoom as a muted preview). Double-bezel with candlelight top highlight. The candlelight glow pools from the upper-right corner. Clean, non-overlapping zones.

3) HOW IT WORKS: a two-column ZIG-ZAG (never three equal cards), three steps alternating text and a cataloged "vitrine" artifact. Step 1 "Submit your plainest news." (paste running-late / PTO / blocked-ticket text) paired with a vitrine of a plain phone-message screenshot behind glass, capped by a Foxed-Edge brass plate reading, in Space Mono, "Plate I - Original Correspondence." Step 2 "We enter it into the record." (rewritten in solemn period voice) paired with a parchment dispatch excerpt in Pinyon Script. Step 3 "It is read aloud, on camera." (a Ken Burns video: period narration, script on parchment, mournful fiddle and banjo, slow pan and zoom) paired with a vitrine of a duotoned period photograph (use picsum.photos, sepia-duotoned) with a slow specular sweep across its glass, brass plate "Plate III - Reading of the Dispatch, c. 1863."

4) EXAMPLES / BEFORE -> AFTER: an asymmetric editorial grid (one large feature beside two smaller - not a row of equal cards), each item a vitrine pairing the mundane original with the parchment dispatch. Use these real dry examples, no fabricated stats: Original "running 10 min late, traffic" -> Dispatch "I write in haste, delayed upon the turnpike by a congestion of wagons; expect my arrival within the quarter-hour, God willing." | Original "OOO next week, taking PTO" -> Dispatch "I am granted leave of the front and shall be absent from all correspondence a se'nnight; direct urgent matters to Corporal Higgins." | Original "the ticket is blocked" -> Dispatch "Our advance is halted; the works before us remain impassable and await reinforcement from the rear." Each original is shown in Space Mono as a cataloged plate with a brass Space Mono catalog number; each dispatch is Pinyon Script on parchment, ending in a statically-depicted, already-pressed seal (register 2). Captions are factual and specific, never jokey.

5) LETTERIFY TRY-IT PANEL: the working tool on a parchment surface. A textarea styled as ruled ledger paper with faint Foxed-Edge horizontal rules and a Space Mono label above it "DISPATCH - DRAFT" and placeholder "Enter your plainest message...". Beneath it, the SIGNATURE control: a large hand-pressed Oxblood wax seal rendered with genuine relief (an irregular edge, a dual cast shadow, an inset candlelight specular where the flame hits the top, and a debossed "SB" monogram), labeled "Press the seal to dispatch" - this is the submit button, and it looks physically pressable. To the right, the output slot shows the resulting rendered dispatch card plus a quiet "View the film" affordance. THIS is the one and only pressable relief wax seal on the page and the only seal that animates - every other seal is either statically-depicted product imagery (the hero card, the hero headline's terminal period, the Examples dispatches) or a small flat "SB" monogram brand glyph (header and footer). There are no loose or decorative seals.

6) FOOTER: a full-bleed "parlor plinth" where the warm parchment darkens into Vignette Umber #140a02 (quoting the film's deep vignette). Warm Bone #e7d6b0 text, a short Fraunces sign-off, a single small FLAT "SB" monogram mark (register 3 - flat, not a relief seal), and a Space Mono colophon line. Quiet and cinematic.

HARD BANS: no emojis; no Inter/system fonts; no generic serifs; never pure #000000; no second accent color; no neon, AI-purple, blue glow, or outer-glow shadows; no oversaturated colors; no centered hero; no three-equal-column card row; no overlapping elements; no two primary CTAs in one view (exactly one Oxblood primary fill per viewport; a persistent nav's call-to-action is always ghost/secondary); no fabricated metrics/uptime/stats; no "LABEL // YEAR"; no AI cliches (Elevate/Seamless/Unleash/Next-Gen); no filler UI ("scroll to explore", bouncing chevrons); no broken image links (use picsum.photos or inline SVG); no torn-paper, burnt edges, heavy sepia wash, western/"WANTED" slab fonts, or clip-art seals; obey the wax seal doctrine - exactly one pressable relief seal (the Letterify submit) that animates, all other seals flat brand glyphs or statically-depicted product imagery. Keep grain subtle (~0.03), keep the paper mostly clean, keep the copy dry and reverent like a documentary narrator who does not realize the letter is about a dentist appointment.
